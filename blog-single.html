Less is More: How Mentoring the Youth Helps Us Build Stronger Communities.

June 10, 2022

Design Mentorship Featured

When one thinks about aiding our communities one often mentions supporting them by offering resources, others may host or throw events but what are the results of that. This may sound like a platitude but it is very true in its essence, "You give a man to fish they eat for a day, but if you teach a man to fish they eat for a lifetime". Our future is often a distant thought. We tend to live in the past and even then its difficult to live in the present. This has been the case with our youth who are often forgotten. More so are the youth at risk who don't have the most positive upbringing. They are often the ones who get neglected and as a result are subjected to rigorious experiences. These are the youth most at need. Those that society has deemed unfit to be part of it. Over my years working with kids and youth at rist, one thing has been certain, they need a figuere a mentor in their lives. They often lack the mentorship necessary to devise an appropriate plan for their future. Even more so, they don't think about the future, just the past, and sometimes the present. It is a vicious cycle that needs to be broken.

When I founded Reynoso's Hit N Walk, I had three core ideas in mind, help my community, mentor the youth, and support small busineses. I saw the need in all three areas, in fact, all those three areas make our communities stronger. However, one of them, the youth, stands above all. They are our future, the ones who will carry on, and take the torch from us. It is our duty to mentor and raise them to become the next leaders of our communities so that they could pay it forward and teach the ones after them.

Youth Leadership

One of the things we can do to help shape our youth is immersing them in different industries that make our economy what it is. This very distictive experience will allow them to learn about the different functioning mechanisms in our society that propel it forward and will expand their horizons. This will utlimately play into their educational experience. For instance, many of our at-risk youth were taken on a tour of a Federal Prison at Terminal Island. Not only did they get to tour the prison from inside, but they also were spoken to by some of the inmates and offered them advise.

It takes a village to raise a child, to raise a child means more than parents helping one another.

African Proverb

We finished that expereince with a bbq at the beach. At the end, many of our mentees came out with a positive outlook and greater motivation to keep on the right track. We had a debrief with many of our mentees after and asked them a simple question, "how do you guys feel now after experiencing what you guys did?" Many of them responded with having a positive experience but also having a much needed figuere in their lives. Many of these individuals don't have the figuere or mentor in their lives that keeps them on the right track. Instead, they seek for that individual an often time it is in the streets.

Mentors

One of the things we started implementing this year has been giving each of our youth leadership students a mentor. This mentor will look out for them and teach them the ropes.We believe it will play a factor in helping them fullfill their potential and accomplish their goals.

We believe the benefits of this program will help shape our youth. Our mentors are people who work in different industries and have a proven track record for leadership. One of things we advise our mentors to do is to catch up and sit with them at least once a week. Some other tasks include the following:

  • Go out for coffee chats.
    • Talk to them and see how they are doing.
    • Help them with their career goals.
    • Connect them to other people.
  • Shadow their jobs.
  • Volunteer with them at different events.

At the end, it truly does take a village to raise a child. One of the last places we visit is the police department. One of the reasons why is so they can get a first hand look at our cities first responders and the things they experience on a daily basis. Many of our mentees come out in awe at the things they learn. They get to talk to the Chief of Police. After that talk he offers them an opportunity to volunteer with them or join their cadet prorgam. This is one of many ways we connect our youth members to their communities. In the end, we need to be there for them so that they take up the torch for the next generation.
